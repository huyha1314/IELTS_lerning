## ⏱ 3. Daily Structure (60 to 75 Minutes / 5 High-Intensity Days + 1 Flex Day)
Since you are balancing high-performance computing, genomic data, and ultramarathon training, we are shifting from passive "Learning" to aggressive "Deploying". Your brain is already analytical. We just need to channel it efficiently.
Every study session is split:
- **First 15-20 mins (Input):** Learn a specific grammar structure, watch a quick strategy video, or review vocabulary.
- **Next 45-55 mins (Output & Deploy):** Immediate practice. Write sentences about bioinformatics or ultramarathons using the new grammar. Do timed IELTS practice. Complete your **ERROR REVIEW** with a heavy focus on aggressive spelling correction. 

> [!WARNING]
> **The Golden Rule**: Doing practice tests without reviewing your mistakes is useless. You must spend as much time reviewing *why* you got an answer wrong as you did answering it. Keep a notebook called the **"Error Log"**.

## 📅 4. Weekly Day-by-Day Schedule (Evolves by Phase)

### 🟢 Phase 1: Foundation & Format (Weeks 1 - 6)
**Goal:** Fix basic grammar, build vocabulary, and understand every part of the IELTS test.
**Materials:** **[B1] Grammar**, **[B2] Vocab**, **[B3] Guide**.

* **Monday (Reading & Vocab)**
  * **Task 1 (15m):** Do 1 Unit of **[B2] Vocab**.
  * **Task 2 (45m):** Take 1 Reading Passage from **[T9] Test 9**. **No time limit.** Focus on getting it right. Review mistakes.
  * **KPI:** Deploy 5 new words in a written summary about your lab work.
* **Tuesday (Listening & Spelling)**
  * **Task 1 (15m):** Listen to 1 episode of **IELTS Speaking for Success**.
  * **Task 2 (45m):** Do 1 Listening section from **[T9] Test 9**. Add all misspelled words to your Error Log.
  * **KPI:** 100% spelling accuracy on your Error Log drill.
* **Wednesday (Flex Day)**
  * **Task:** If your workload is overwhelming today, use this Flex Day to recover. If you have energy, do a 30-minute review of your Error Log.
* **Thursday (Writing Task 1 & Grammar)**
  * **Task 1 (15m):** Learn 1 complex grammar structure (e.g., mixed conditionals) from **[B1] Grammar**.
  * **Task 2 (45m):** Watch an **IELTS Liz** video on Writing Task 1, then write 1 practice Task 1 report.
  * **KPI:** Write 3 sentences about genomic datasets using your new complex grammar structure.
* **Friday (Writing Task 2 Focus)**
  * **Task 1 (15m):** Watch an **IELTS Advantage** YouTube video on Writing Task 2 structure.
  * **Task 2 (60m):** Write one full Task 2 essay applying the video's strategy.
  * **KPI:** Complete a 250-word essay. Aggressively proofread for typos.
* **Saturday (Speaking & Active Listening)**
  * **Task 1 (20m):** Practice Speaking Part 1 questions out loud. **Record yourself**.
  * **Task 2 (40m):** Listen to your recording. Transcribe your exact words. Correct your own grammar.
  * **KPI:** Speak for 2 minutes. Correct 3 of your own spoken errors.
* **Sunday (Rest & Reevaluate)**
  * **Review:** Look at your Error Log. Relax completely to recover cognitive energy.

### 🟡 Phase 2: Core Strategies & Timed Practice (Weeks 7 - 14)
**Goal:** Apply advanced strategies, improve speed, start doing timed practice.
**Materials:** *Target Band 7 (Simone Braverman)*, *Cambridge IELTS 10 - 13*.

* **Monday (Advanced Reading Strategy)**
  * **Task 1 (30m):** Read Reading Strategies in *Target Band 7*.
  * **Task 2 (75m):** Do 2 Reading Passages from *Cambridge IELTS 10* under strict time limits (40 mins). Spend 35 mins reviewing errors.
  * **KPI:** Finish 2 passages in 40 mins. Understand *why* you got True/False/Not Given wrong.
* **Tuesday (Listening Section 3 & 4)**
  * **Task 1 (30m):** Read Listening Strategies in *Target Band 7*.
  * **Task 2 (75m):** Do a full Listening Test (*Cambridge IELTS 10*). Transcribe (write down word-for-word) any sentences you couldn't understand.
  * **KPI:** Score 26+ (Band 6.5 level). 
* **Wednesday (Writing Task 1 & 2)**
  * **Task 1 (45m):** Watch an **IELTS Liz** video on Writing Task 1 (e.g., maps or pie charts) and read Writing Strategies in *Target Band 7*. 
  * **Task 2 (60m):** Write 1 Task 1 and 1 Introduction/Conclusion for Task 2. 
  * **KPI:** Write Task 1 in 20 minutes exactly.
* **Thursday (Reading Full Test)**
  * **Task (105m):** Do a FULL Reading test (3 passages) in 60 minutes from *Cambridge IELTS 11*. Spend 45 minutes analyzing mistakes in your Error Log.
  * **KPI:** Complete all 40 questions in 60 mins. Score 27+.
* **Friday (Writing Task 2 Focus)**
  * **Task (105m):** Write 1 full Task 2 essay (40 mins). Spend the rest of the time putting your essay into an AI (like ChatGPT or me) asking for a Band score and grammar corrections. Rewrite the essay perfectly.
  * **KPI:** Use 3 advanced vocabulary words and 2 complex grammar structures (e.g., conditional, relative clause).
* **Saturday (Speaking Parts 2 & 3)**
  * **Task 1 (45m):** Watch a Band 7.5+ mock Speaking test on **E2 IELTS** or **IELTS Advantage** on YouTube. Take notes on their idioms.
  * **Task 2 (60m):** Prepare notes for 3 different Speaking Part 2 topics. Record yourself. Do Speaking Part 3 with an AI voice feature or language partner.
  * **KPI:** Speak fluently without saying "um" or "ah" too much. Use 2 new idioms.
* **Sunday (Rest & Reevaluate)**
  * **Review KPI:** Are your reading scores improving? What is your weakest question type? (e.g., Matching Headings). Plan to study that type next week.

* **Friday:** Write 1 Full Task 2 (40m). Focus on complex grammar and high-level vocabulary from your *Oxford Word Skills* book.
* **Saturday:** Full Mock Speaking Test (15m). Review recording (15m). Do 1 Full Listening Test (40m).
* **Sunday (Rest & Reevaluate):** Review overall progress. Are you consistently hitting 30/40 on Listening and Reading?
