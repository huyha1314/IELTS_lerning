# 🎮 The IELTS Vocab Game: High-Efficiency System

Since you are incredibly busy with genomics and ultramarathon training, traditional "sit down and read a dictionary" vocabulary learning will fail. We need a system that takes **0 extra hours out of your day**. 

We are going to gamify your vocabulary using **"Micro-Quests"** (5-10 minute bursts) and the neuroscience of sleep.

---

## 🛠️ The Core Engine: Anki (Spaced Repetition)
Download the app **Anki** (or Quizlet) on your phone. This algorithm tracks exactly when you are about to forget a word and forces you to review it.
*   **Rule:** You never study Anki at your desk. You ONLY play Anki during "dead time": waiting for a genomic dataset to compile, sitting on the toilet, or waiting for a coffee. 

---

## 🗺️ The Weekly Quest Board

Treat every week like a new level in an RPG. Your goal is to gather "Loot" (words) and use them to defeat the "Boss" (the IELTS Examiner).

### Phase 1: Looting (Mon - Wed)
*   **The Mission:** Extract 15 high-level words from your *Oxford Word Skills* book or your reading practice.
*   **The Rule:** Do not just add the word to Anki. Add the **sentence** you found it in. (e.g., Front of card: *The data showed a significant [drop] in variants.* Back: *plunge*).

### Phase 2: The Forge (Thu - Fri)
*   **The Mission:** You cannot just memorize; you must forge the weapon (deploy the word). 
*   **The Micro-Quest:** Pick 3 words from your Anki deck. You **MUST** use them in a real-life conversation, a Slack message to your lab team, or in an email today. Even if it sounds slightly formal, force it into your real life.

### Phase 3: The Boss Fight (Saturday)
*   **The Mission:** Your IELTS Writing and Speaking practice.
*   **The Rule:** Before you start your essay or speaking recording, write 5 of your weekly words at the top of the page. You cannot finish the "Boss Fight" until you have used all 5 words naturally.

---

## ⚡ 10-Minute "Stealth" Quests (For Nap Time & Running)

Here is how you sneak vocabulary into your day without burning cognitive energy:

### 1. The "Nap-Time Memory Hack" (10 Minutes)
*   **The Science:** Your brain consolidates memory while you sleep. Learning right before a nap is highly efficient.
*   **The Quest:** Right before you close your eyes for a nap or for the night, do exactly **5 minutes of Anki**. When you wake up, spend **2 minutes** writing down as many words as you can remember from before you slept.

### 2. The "Ultramarathon Shadow"
*   **The Science:** Physical movement increases blood flow to the hippocampus (the memory center of the brain).
*   **The Quest:** While you are on a long training run, listen to *IELTS Speaking for Success*. When you hear a great vocabulary word, **repeat it out loud** while running. Say a full sentence using the word. Yes, you will look a bit crazy, but it locks the word into your muscle memory.

### 3. The Monitor "Sticky Bomb"
*   **The Quest:** Take a physical sticky note. Write your 3 hardest words of the week on it. Stick it to the corner of your lab monitor where you process your genomic data. You are not allowed to take the sticky note down until you have successfully used those 3 words in a sentence that day.

---

## 🏆 Scoring & Rewards

*   **+10 XP:** Adding a new word to Anki with a genomic/running example sentence.
*   **+50 XP:** Successfully sneaking an IELTS vocabulary word into a real conversation with a colleague.
*   **+100 XP (Boss Defeated):** Using 5 weekly words correctly in your Saturday mock essay.

If you hit **500 XP** by Sunday, you unlock your Macro-Reward (a massive cheat meal, a guilt-free movie, or buying new running gear). Game on!
